SURVEY QUESTIONNAIRE (GOVCONNECT)


A. General Background

1. Kindly describe the mandate and primary functions of the LGU Caba?

The LGU-Caba, La Union is mandated to deliver basic services to its constituents, promote general welfare, and foster sustainable development. Its primary functions include local governance, public safety, health and social services, agriculture and fisheries support, education, disaster risk reduction and management, livelihood assistance, infrastructure development, and the promotion of local culture and tourism.

2. What are the current priorities or flagship programs of the municipality?

The top priorities of flagship programs of the municipality includes the following 
•	Health services (improved RHU and health care services, expanded health facilities, anti-drug programs, nutrition programs) 
•	Social protection programs (livelihood, AICS/TUPAD, assistance for farmers, fisherfolk, senior citizens, PWDs, indigents and all sectors especially the vulnerable)
•	Infrastructure development (Farm-to-Market Roads, Slope protection, water system, disaster-resilient public infrastructures, buildings and facilities) 
•	Peace and order and public safety (anti-criminality campaigns, strengthening MPOC-MADAC, anti-drug advocacies, crime prevention, disaster response, community safety)
•	Environmental management (coastal protection, solid waste management, climate adaptation) 
•	Digitalization and transparency (improving records management, online presence, and e-governance) 
•	Education (scholarships, youth development programs, school support, literacy campaigns)
•	Sustainable Tourism promotion and culture and arts preservation (annual fiesta, cultural heritage, bamboo-related initiatives)

B. Inventory of LGU Services

3. May we request a list of the offices/departments under LGU Caba and their key functions?

1. Office of the Mayor
•	Exercises general supervision and control over all LGU departments and offices.
•	Implements municipal laws, ordinances, and policies.
•	Provides executive leadership in governance, development planning, peace and order, and public welfare programs.
2. Office of the Vice Mayor & Sangguniang Bayan
•	Enacts ordinances and resolutions for local governance.
•	Approves the annual budget and development plans.
•	Exercises oversight on executive implementation of policies and programs.
3. Office of the Municipal Budget Officer
•	Prepares the annual municipal budget and supplemental budgets.
•	Monitors and evaluates budget implementation.
•	Ensures budget allocation supports development priorities and legal requirements.
4. Office of the Municipal Accountant
•	Maintains and prepares financial statements and reports.
•	Ensures proper recording of disbursements and obligations.
•	Certifies the availability of funds for LGU transactions.
5. Office of the Municipal Treasurer
•	Collects taxes, fees, and other revenues of the municipality.
•	Manages custody of municipal funds and other financial assets.
•	Ensures efficient cash management and disbursement of public funds.
6. Office of the Municipal Civil Registrar
•	Registers vital events such as births, deaths, and marriages.
•	Issues certified copies of civil registry documents.
•	Maintains and safeguards civil registry records.
7. Office of the Municipal Assessor
•	Appraises and assesses real properties within the municipality for taxation purposes.
•	Prepares the schedule of market values.
•	Maintains updated records of property ownership and classification.
8. Office of the Municipal Engineer
•	Prepares and supervises implementation of infrastructure projects.
•	Provides technical support on public works, water systems, and facilities.
•	Ensures compliance with building codes and regulations.
9. Office of the Municipal Agriculturist
•	Promotes agricultural and fisheries development.
•	Provides technical assistance, training, and support services to farmers and fisherfolk.
•	Implements programs on food security and sustainable farming.
10. Office of the Municipal Health Officer
•	Delivers primary health care, immunization, and medical services.
•	Implements public health programs (nutrition, sanitation, disease prevention).
•	Supervises operations of the Rural Health Unit (RHU).
11. Office of the Municipal Planning and Development Officer (MPDO)
•	Formulates comprehensive development and land use plans.
•	Prepares investment programs and monitors project implementation.
•	Provides data and research support for decision-making.
12. Office of the Municipal Social Welfare and Development Officer (MSWDO)
•	Implements social protection and welfare programs.
•	Provides financial assistance to vulnerable groups (indigents, PWDs, senior citizens, children-at-risk).
•	Conducts case management, relief, and rehabilitation services.
13. Office of the Local Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Officer (MDRRMO)
•	Formulates and implements the Local DRRM Plan.
•	Leads preparedness, response, recovery, and rehabilitation activities.
•	Coordinates emergency operations and ensures community resilience.
14. Office of the Mayor – Human Resource Management Office (HRMO)
•	Manages recruitment, selection, and placement of personnel.
•	Oversees employee welfare, training, and performance evaluation.
•	Ensures compliance with civil service laws and rules.
15. Office of the Mayor – Business Permits and Licensing Office (BPLO)
•	Facilitates the issuance and renewal of business permits and licenses.
•	Ensures compliance with regulatory requirements.
•	Maintains records of registered businesses and promotes ease of doing business.
16. Office of the Mayor – General Services Office (GSO)
•	Manages procurement, custody, and maintenance of LGU supplies, equipment, and properties.
•	Oversees waste management and logistical support services.
•	Ensures efficient use and safekeeping of government resources.

17. Office of the Mayor – Municipal Tourism, Culture and the Arts Office (MTCAO)

•	Promotes tourism programs and cultural heritage preservation.
•	Plans and implements events, festivals, and cultural activities.
•	Develops tourism packages and partnerships to boost local economy.

4. What services does the LGU currently provide to residents? (e.g., civil registry, health services, agriculture, social welfare, business permits, disaster management, etc.)

The different services are: 
•	Civil registry services (birth, marriage, death certificates)
•	Issuance of building permits, business permits, licenses, and clearances and other related documents
•	Health services (check-ups, immunizations, RHU services, medical missions)
•	Social welfare services (financial assistance, relief goods, social case studies, rehabilitation referrals)
•	Livelihood programs and farmer/fisherfolk support and assistance
•	Solid waste collection and management
•	Disaster response and relief operations
•	Infrastructure development and maintenance
•	Educational support (scholarships, learning materials, youth development)
•	Tourism promotion and cultural activities.

C. Tourism and Local Assets

6. What are the key tourist attractions, natural landmarks, or cultural sites in Caba?

•	Caba’s beaches (surfing, swimming, coastal recreation)
•	Caba Farmer’s Agri-tourism Promotions Facility & Diego Silang Civic Center and Historical Park and Eco-tourism Plaza
•	Diego Silang Monument and President Elpidio Quirino Monument
•	Caba-Naguilian Road (gateway to Baguio)
•	Local bamboo products and crafts
•	Annual Caba Town Fiesta and Bamboo Festival
•	Wenceslao Acacia Centennial Tree
•	Grapes Farms and Mulberry Farm – Wenceslao, Urayong-Caba
•	Mount Kimmalugong - Sobredillo
•	Tapao - Urayong
•	Iglesia Filipina Independiente Parish Church (Spanish-era)
•	Nagsimabaanan (oldest catholic church) 

7. Does the municipality organize annual festivals, cultural events, or fairs that should be promoted through the website?

Yes. Caba celebrates its Annual Town Fiesta and Bamboo Festival every December, featuring cultural shows, parades, religious activities, agri-trade fairs and community events. The LGU also organizes sports fests, youth summits, and environmental activities.

8. What are the local products, delicacies, or crafts that represent Caba's identity?

•	Bamboo products and crafts
•	Fresh seafood and farm produce (fish, vegetables, root crops)
•	Fruit vinegars (guyabano, banana, pineapple)
•	San Jose MPC products (differently flavored pastillas, polvoron, and other sweets)
•	Chips (cassava, camote, banana)
•	Sweet pineapple
•	Lansones

15. Which groups do you think face the most difficulty accessing LGU information and services today?

F. Communication and Information Management

16. What communication channels are currently used (bulletin boards, social media, radio, etc.)?

•	Bulletin boards in barangays
•	Official Facebook page of LGU-Caba
•	Word-of-mouth announcements via barangay officials
•	Text brigade during emergencies
•	Public assemblies and barangay meetings
•	Local radio partnerships (occasionally).
17. Are there existing challenges in keeping the public updated on policies, ordinances, or activities?

•	Poor internet connectivity in remote areas
•	Limited manpower and facilities for information management
•	Not all residents are active on social media
•	Lack of a centralized official website.

18. How does the LGU currently handle public feedback, complaints, or inquiries?

•	Through the LGU offices especially Mayor’s Office
•	Barangay officials forwarding concerns
•	Walk-in clients at LGU offices (clients feedback)
•	Social media messages on the official LGU page.

H. Practical Considerations


21. Who in the LGU would likely manage and update the website?

The ICT focal person or the HRMO in coordination with the Municipal Information and Tourism Officer, MPDO, LDRRMO and the designated personnel per office as representatives for content updates.


22. What challenges do you foresee in sustaining the website (e.g., budget, manpower, training. internet connectivity)?

•	Limited budget for hosting and maintenance
•	Need for regular training of staff in-charge
•	Ensuring fast and reliable internet connectivity
•	Dedicated manpower for updates and monitoring.

I. Closing Insights

23. Why do you think LGU Caba needs an official website at this time?

•	To strengthen transparency and good governance
•	To improve public access to services and information
•	To promote tourism and local products
•	To centralize official announcements/advisories/updates, reducing misinformation.

24. What is your vision for a municipal website that truly reflects Caba's identity and serves its people?

A user-friendly, reliable, and transparent platform that reflects Caba’s identity, culture, and progress—serving as a bridge between the LGU and its people while showcasing the municipality’s tourism, heritage, opportunities, and development for both residents and visitors.
